The Logistics Share this

  • This camp runs Tuesday – Thursday (March 15th – March 17th).
  • Morning sessions will start at 10:00 am and last 2 hours.
  • Afternoon sessions will start at 12:30 pm and last 2 hours.
  • All sessions will be held at the McMaster Sports Complex near Bark Park (directions).
  • Max six to one player to coach ratio.
  • Max ten players per court.
  • Skill Level: Beginner – Advanced
  • Coaches: Chris Olds & Tania Lyerly

The Features

  • Every session will feature progressive drills focused on the specific mechanics & strategies of beach volleyball.
  • The players will work through competitive cauldron scenarios where athletes can implement their newfound skills in game-like situations.
  • We'll give everyone drills to work on at home individually and with each other to maximize their improvement.
  • Depending on the drill, players will be divided into separate skill groups and/or mixed together. This allows the stronger players work with and against each other, as well as learn how to help out a teammate when necessary. Newer players get to improve with each other, and also have an opportunity to learn from the more experienced players.
  • If you already have a tournament partner, just let us know, and we'll pair you together for most drills. If not, we'll help you find one.
  • You can choose to attend Mornings, Afternoons, or both.
  • For those coming all day, we'll take a half hour break at noon for lunch. We'll provide some free snacks (granola bars, fruit snacks, etc.), but players should bring a sandwich or something if they need more fuel before the afternoon session.

Bring a Friend

You can save on the cost of the camp by inviting a friend to come with you to whichever camp you'll be at (Mornings, Afternoons, or Both). The Bring a Friend price is the total cost for both players. It's up to you to collect your friend's half of the payment, unless you just feel like giving them a present. :)

Notes

  • Bring plenty of water.
  • Don't forget to wear sunscreen!
  • After you register, you'll be redirected to a Player Information form. This form helps us get a better idea of what you're looking to get out of training, & how we can best help you achieve your goals.
  • If you choose to Bring a Friend, please send the link to the Player Information form to your friend, so they can get the most out of training, too.

About Your Coaches Passionate Coaches Who Care

Chris Olds Director / Coach

  • 5 years Playing & Coaching Experience

Chris fell in love with beach volleyball shortly after moving to Austin in 2011. He started spending less & less time playing basketball (his first love), and more & more time mastering the beach game (always a work in progress). He quickly worked his way up the ranks, making it to the finals in 30+ tournaments & winning 17+ times in just a couple years. He earned his first main draw berth in the NVL less than 3 years after he started playing the sport.

Chris has helped build several of the beach programs around the Austin Area. Now he's focused on building 512 Beach & delivering the best experience possible. He is also the 2A Volleyball Head Coach at St. Andrew’s Middle School.

Fun Fact: Chris scored the golden goal in sudden death overtime in the 2007 Korfball World Cup, leading Team USA to their first victory in 16 years. What's korfball...? Feel free to ask him.

Certifications: USAV Impact, USAV SafeSport, USAV Background Check

If you aren't enjoying the game, what's the point? I love having fun with the players I coach and watching them improve day by day. My coaching drive comes from seeing those lightbulb moments & the sparkle in a player's eyes when everything just clicks... and then the determination to do it again.

Tania Lyerly Director / Coach

  • 4 years Playing & Coaching Experience

Tania loves helping teens succeed. She helps student-athletes as an ACT/SAT tutor by day, and as volleyball coach by night (and day...). She's also our resident college recruiting specialist, helping athletes & parents navigate the road to college, whether it's through athletics, academics, or a combination of the two. It doesn't hurt that she's been through it all before herself, when she was a scholarship athlete at Northwestern University as a diver.

Beyond Volleyball... More Volleyball: Tania currently plays beach volleyball, based out of Austin, TX. She has been the Texas Volleyball Tour (TVT) Women’s BB points leader for the last 2 years, and won the 2015 Women’s BB Texas Volleyball Tour end of season Championship. During this off season she is training to be ready to compete at the AA level next year.

Fun Fact: Tania was in a circus! Ask her about it sometime. :)

Certifications: USAV Impact, USAV SafeSport, USAV Background Check

Volleyball is fun! (And hard work.) It provides a break from the outside world. Volleyball teaches valuable life skills: teamwork, time management, communication, dedication, and the ability to overcome failures. I believe it’s a coach’s role to develop good people with exceptional life skills, because those make the best players and teammates.
