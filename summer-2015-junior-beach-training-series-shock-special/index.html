The Logistics

  • The Summer Training Series runs for five weeks (June 8th – July 8th).
  • Each session will start at 8:00 am and last 90 minutes.
  • All training sessions are centrally located at Krieg Field Volleyball Courts (directions).
  • Max six to one player to coach ratio.
  • Max ten players per court.
  • Each package is limited to the first 10 players to sign up. They'll go quick, so act fast!

The Features

  • Every session will feature progressive drills focused on the specific mechanics & strategies of beach volleyball.
  • We'll put the players in competitive cauldron scenarios where athletes can implement their newfound skills in game-like situations.
  • Every player will receive a free official Wilson beach volleyball.
  • We'll give everyone drills they can work on at home individually with their new ball and with each other to maximize their improvement each week and after they've completed the Summer Training Series.
  • No tryouts. Players will both be divided into separate skill groups as well mixed together so the stronger players can work with the beginners to improve their own abilities as well as the beginners'.
  • If you already have a tournament partner, just let us know, and we'll pair you together for most drills. If not, we'll help you find one.
  • You can choose to train on Mondays, Wednesdays, or both days each week.

Our Coaching Philosophy

We strive to use our time efficiently. The first part of each practice is dedicated to focusing on passing, setting, and hitting mechanics. We then use drills to reinforce those mechanics in more game-like situations.

Our training objectives are:

  1. Make volleyball fun
  2. Prepare for the next upcoming tournament
  3. Improve teamwork and reinforce the importance of this
  4. Improve individual skill sets

We choose to coach this way because we believe:

  1. If players aren't having fun, they're missing the point of the game.
  2. If players don't feel competitive, they might give up. Tournament wins help drive competitiveness.
  3. If players aren't good at being part of a team, they won't succeed in volleyball regardless of their mechanical prowess.
  4. Skills should improve from coaching and the prior three points due to it being fun and players realizing that their individual skill sets help in succeeding at volleyball.

About Your Coaches

Zack Kweder and Chris Olds are the Head Coaches. We've been playing and coaching together since 2012. We're actively traveling and competing on the NVL Beach Volleyball Tour. We're currently ranked 42nd and 51st on the national tour, and we're hoping to make this our best year yet.

If you've ever met Zack, you know he's all smiles. He has a lot to say and loves sharing his knowledge and helping players improve.

Chris doesn't always talk as much as Zack, but when he does — it's usually worth listening to. :)

We provide a good balance for each other, because we have complimentary styles, and we both strive to help our players develop their game in the best way possible. Just look at our coaching philosophy above to see what we believe.

We coach both indoor and sand, but our hearts are definitely on the beach. The indoor coaching experience has enabled us to better help players transitioning from indoor to sand. We like helping indoor players figure out how to translate the skills they already have to make them successful on the beach.

We also pull in a rotating series of third coaches from time to time. They provide a fresh perspective and new voices for the players to respond to.
