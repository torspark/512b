FAQ's

How does Sideout work?

  • 5-6 players on each court.
  • The receiving side is the "King" side.
  • Players keep track of their individual points earned on the King side (aka: every time they sideout).
  • If the serving team wins the rally, they move to the receiving side & receive serve from the next team in line.
    • The original receiving team that lost the rally is off & moves to the back of the line on the serving side.
  • If the receiving team wins the rally, they each earn a point & stay on the court in serve receive again.
    • The non-server from the serving team goes to the back of the line, & the server moves into the court, & the next player in line is the new server.
  • If a server misses their serve, they move to the end of the line, & nobody earns any points.

How does the ladder work?

  • We'll have all four courts running at once for a series of timed rounds.
  • After each round, the top 2 players from each court will move up a rung, & the bottom 2 players from each court will move down a rung.
    • The middle player (or 2) will stay on their court/rung.
    • Everyone gets to keep playing somewhere, unlike the video below.
  • This will give everyone an opportunity to play together & mix up partnerships, while also filtering the stronger players toward the top.
  • Each week players will start where they left off the previous week, giving everyone a chance to hold court &/or work their way up.

What if I can't make it one week? What's the sub policy?

  • Please try to find a sub with comparable skill level, and let us know!
  • If you can't find a sub, you'll drop one rung for each week you miss.
