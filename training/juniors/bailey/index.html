More Details

What's the format of each session?

Sessions are 90 minutes each.

We'll work on specific skills for most of the session, ending with focused gameplay to give players an opportunity to execute everything they've been practicing each day.

We encourage players to get there a little early to warm up before we get started, so we can make the most of our time on the court. But, we'll still start with a low-intensity drill each time, incase anyone can't get there until right on time.

How are the sessions customized? What skills are covered?

Each session will be tailored to those who attend based on Player Assessments & any questions/topics brought up during training.

We'll be working on a variety of skills (Passing, Setting, Attacking, Defense, Blocking, Serving, & Game Theory/Strategy), depending on who attends each session & what the players need most.

If you're looking for help with something specific, just let us know, & we'll try to work it into the next session.

Will beach volleyball help my indoor game?

Let's let some of the most influential people in the sport take this one:

It’s easier to take your outdoor skills into the gym than to bring your indoor skills outside. Simple reason. Beach volleyball forces you to have all the skills. That’s why I think it’s a good idea for indoor players to play on the beach.... Learning those skills can only help improve a player’s indoor performance. All my years playing on the beach as a kid certainly helped my indoor game. If nothing else, a player’s quickness and jump will be increased by playing on soft sand. Karch Kiraly (Indoor & Beach Volleyball Olympic Gold Medalist, USA Women's National Volleyball Team Head Coach)
The beach game is GREAT for improving your indoor skills/game. Whatever your weaknesses are, you get to work on them a ton. Unlike the 6 person game, you touch the ball in every rally, and with just two of you covering the court, you learn to read and anticipate much better. Dealing with the sun and wind helps you be more adaptable. Player height is less important outdoors – ball control and skill is more important... Most top level coaches encourage their players to play as much as they can on the beach. John Kessel (USA Volleyball Director of Sport Development, Grassroots, Disabled Volleyball, and Beach Volleyball, 2013 American Volleyball Coaches Association Coaches Hall of Fame Inductee)

What do you mean by "Instant Video Feedback"?

When the structure of a drill allows, we set up a video feed on a 10-second loop, so players can actually see how they play. We've found that this helps players truly recognize & improve their mechanics, rather than just going through the motions.

How many players?

We cap it at 8 players per session to ensure everyone gets enough quality touches & feedback.

How many coaches?

We'll have at least 2 coaches at almost every session. This lets us give a lot of feedback and divide groups up as necessary.

What if a session is full?

If a session is already full, we'll be in touch to reschedule or give you a refund.

What if it's raining?

A little rain won't stop us, since tournaments are played through rain, too. But, if it's too heavy and/or if there's lightning nearby, we'll either delay or cancel the session.

We'll post weather delay/cancelation updates on Twitter (@512beachvb).

What if a session gets canceled?

If a session needs to be canceled, we'll schedule a makeup date and time, or give you a prorated refund for that session.

What if I need to cancel my subscription?

Just let us know if your schedule changes, and you can no longer attend. This is a rolling month-to-month program, so we'll just cancel your subscription before your next payment is due.
