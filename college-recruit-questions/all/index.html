We've crafted 83 questions to help you know what to ask Colleges & Coaches when they come knocking on your door. Our notes and comments are written in italics.

We've seen a lot of families wait too long to start thinking critically about what they want out of college. You'll be able to answer some of these questions with a little research, while others will need a conversation with the Coach and/or college representatives.

We hope these questions prompt you to plan ahead and really think about what you want to get out of college. Hopefully can find the college program that's the best fit for you.

Good luck in your search!
-- 512 Beach

The Coach/Coaching Staff

Back to Top
  1. What is your coaching philosophy?

  2. What is your coaching style?

  3. Do you consider yourself more of an indoor oriented coach or a beach oriented coach?
    Some programs have the same coach for both indoor & beach volleyball.

  4. How long have you been coaching beach volleyball?

  5. Have you coached indoor? If yes, how long?
    You want a coach who understands the differences between indoor and beach, especially if you are being recruited to play both.

  6. What are your future coaching plans/aspirations?
    Some coaches use weaker/newer programs to make a name for themselves to then move on to a “power house” program. Ideally, you’ll want a coach who will stay with you.

The Program

Back to Top
  1. What are your short term goals for this program?
    Next 1-4 years.

  2. Do you have long terms goals for this program? What are they?
    5-10+ years.

  3. How many current freshman, sophomores, juniors, & seniors are in the program?
    Mostly upperclassman or a younger team with mostly underclassmen. There are benefits to both.

  4. Where do you see me fitting in with those goals? In what capacity?

  5. Why do you think I'm a good fit for your program?

Practice

Back to Top
  1. When does the season start?

  2. What does a typical in-season week look like?
    Practices, weights, game film review, number of competition days, etc.

  3. Can you walk me through a typical practice?

  4. Are there voluntary practices? How often?
    NCAA mandates that no more than 20 hours of practice can be mandated week in-season and 8 hours off-season. Some schools work around this with voluntary practices.

  5. When am I expected to be on campus/move-in?
    • Is this different than the general student population?

  6. When does the pre-season start?

  7. What is the practice schedule?
    • What is the practice schedule as a whole team vs individually?

  8. What are the chances of a post season?

  9. When is the off season?

  10. How are practices different in the off season?

  11. Are there any additional training periods?
    Post Season, Winter Break, Spring Break, etc.

  12. Are there standard practice hours and days for the team?
    Example: Monday – Friday, 2pm – 5pm. NCAA limits practices to a maximum of 4 hours per day in season.

  13. What are my off-season responsibilities?

  14. What is the practice/training policy for student athletes during the summer?
    • Train with the team?
    • Train at home?

  15. What is the winter break training/practice schedule?
    Many schools have practices through winter break that you are expected to attend. Going home may not be an option.

Competition & Travel

Back to Top
  1. What is the team’s past record?

  2. How do you feel about that record?

  3. How many competition days per season?

  4. How many of those are travel days?

  5. How many girls make up the travel team?

  6. How is the travel team determined?

  7. Do you see it fluctuating during the season?
    • Under what conditions?

Recruiting

Back to Top
  1. How many freshmen are being recruited?

  2. How many girls are in the program?

  3. Where do you see me on the depth chart?
    • Coming into the program?
    • In the future?

  4. Am I being recruited exclusively for beach?
    Some schools may recruit you for indoor and beach, but expect you to be primarily a beach player.

  5. Are you considering me for an official recruiting trip?
    Only ask this question if you are interested in taking an Official Visit. Remember, you only get 5 Official Visits! You can take unlimited Unofficial Visits.

  6. Am I being considered for a red shirt? If yes, why?

  7. What are my chances of competing at this point?

  8. Do you see me having a place on the travel team?

  9. If no, when do you see me being able to compete?
    • To travel?

  10. Am I being considered for a scholarship/financial aid?
    Very few schools offer full rides. Instead, coaches will offer partial scholarships, which may include tuition, books, living stipend, meals, etc. Some coaches will give walk-ons scholarships later if they deserve/earn it. Some coaches say they will, but don’t. Find out if that coach has a history of giving scholarships to walk-ons that deserve it.
    • If yes, how much?
    • If no, as a walk-on what are my chances of earning a scholarship in the future?

  11. Which signing period do you foresee me signing during?
    There are 2 signing periods, early in the fall and later in the spring.

  12. Are you expecting a verbal commitment from me?
    • If yes, by when?

  13. Is there anything special that I need to do when I apply to the school?
    Coaches often flag their recruits during the application process. Some athletes may be offered a special coded application depending on the situation.

Academics

Back to Top
  1. Am I qualified to meet admission standards?
    Core Classes + GPA + Test Scores. If you have any Ds in your core classes, you should consider retaking them.

  2. Am I considered “at risk” academically?
    If your grades and/or scores are low, you may be required to have additional academic support, such as additional mandatory study hours and/or tutors.

  3. What academic support is in place to make sure I’m successful?
    • Athletic Advisor?
    • Tutors?
    • Required study hall hours?
    • Academic mentors?

  4. Will I have an academic/major advisor who knows the demands of my sport as well?

  5. On average, how many days of class are missed due to travel?

  6. How supportive are the faculty in allowing me to make up classwork and tests due to the team's competition schedule?
    • What are the on the road vs not on the road make-up options?

  7. What support within the athletic department is given to help me make up tests/missed work?

  8. Will I be steered clear of professors/faculty that are not supportive of athletes?

  9. What happens if I have a required class that conflicts with practice time?
    NCAA Bylaw 17.1.5.5.1 No Class Time Missed for Practice Activities.

  10. What are “typical” majors of girls on the team?
    Some schools/coaches discourage “harder” majors because of academic demands being a distraction from athletics. If a coach expects you to sacrifice academics for athletics, that may not be in your best interest post college.
    • Of student-athletes at the school?

  11. Who is involved in helping me determine my major/classes?
    Athletic advisor, academic advisor?

  12. What are the requirements for the honors program?

  13. What percentage of student-athletes are in the honors program/qualify for honors?

  14. How does class selection and registration work?
    Student-athletes are often given priority class registration of some kind to work around practice schedules.

  15. Do most athletes graduate with a major, major and minor, double major, major and two minors, or some other combination?
    Some schools make it is easier to double major than others, if you're interested in doing so.

  16. What percentage of student-athletes graduate?
    • In 4 years vs 5+ years?

  17. What percentage of beach/indoor volleyball players graduate?
    • In 4 years vs 5+ years?

  18. How do these percentages compare to the general undergraduate population?

  19. How many credits/units are required for me to be eligible to compete each year? To graduate?

  20. Are the coaches supportive if I have academic problems or need additional academic support?

Campus Life

Back to Top
  1. What are housing arrangements for freshmen athletes?

  2. What are typical housing arrangements after freshman year?
    Most schools require students to live on campus the first year, sometimes longer, and usually you’ll room with a teammate. After your freshman year, you may have the option to move off campus.

  3. When is my move-in date?
    Move-in for student-athletes is often different for general student population.

  4. What is your (coach’s) policy on players being involved with other campus organizations other than the team/athletics?
    Some coaches are supportive of athletes being involved in Greek life, or other activities. Some coaches are not.

  5. Can I have a car on-campus? Off campus?
    Freshmen rarely have cars on campus, but that may be different for later years.

Financial

Back to Top
  1. Is there academic/need based financial aid available?

  2. Do I qualify for either? Both? How much?

  3. Are there any grants or other special circumstances that qualify me for financial support?

  4. Is it a one time or multi-year financial award?

  5. How many years is the multi-year aid available for?

  6. What criteria are used to determine renewable aid?
    Example: Minimum academic requirements?

  7. What expenses would the financial support cover?
    ie. books, room, tuition, meals, etc.

  8. If I need 5 years to graduate, will I continue to receive the same amount of financial support as the other 4 years?

Legal

Back to Top
  1. What medical expenses are covered by the university?

  2. If I get injured as a result of my sport and am unable to compete, will I lose my financial support/scholarship?

  3. What is the status of the college’s relationship with the NCAA?
    Are there any current or upcoming sanctions against the program?
    • Other governing bodies?
